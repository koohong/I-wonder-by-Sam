  • Created by spanning a vector or set of vectors

  • Has orthogonal complement subspace (they are like best friends)

C and P

\[\begin{aligned} _{n}P_{r} &= \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}\\ _{n}C_{r} &= \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} \end{aligned}\]

Another way to express combination \[{N\choose r} = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!r!}\]
